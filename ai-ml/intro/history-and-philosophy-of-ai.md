## Description

This document traces the philosophical and historical roots of AI — from ancient myths to modern deep learning — and explores the foundational questions that every AI practitioner should understand.

## Content / Material

### The Dream of Intelligent Machines

The idea of artificial intelligence is not new:

- **Ancient Greece** — Hephaestus created mechanical servants in myth.
- **1300s** — Ramon Llull designed "thinking machines" for combining concepts.
- **1600s** — Leibniz dreamed of a *characteristica universalis* — a universal language of reasoning.
- **1950** — Turing published "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" proposing the Imitation Game.
- **1956** — Dartmouth Conference coins the term "Artificial Intelligence."

### Key Philosophical Questions

| Question | What It Means |
|----------|---------------|
| **Can machines think?** | Is intelligence unique to biological systems? |
| **The Chinese Room** | Does a machine that manipulates symbols *understand* them? |
| **The Hard Problem** | Can a machine be conscious, or just simulate consciousness? |
| **Alignment** | How do we ensure AI goals match human values? |

These are not academic curiosities — they shape how we design, deploy, and regulate AI systems.

### The First AI Winter to the Deep Learning Revolution

AI has experienced cycles of hype and disappointment:

| Era | Key Development | Outcome |
|-----|----------------|---------|
| 1956–1974 | Symbolic AI, logic, search | Overpromised, underdelivered → AI Winter |
| 1980s | Expert systems | Too brittle, hard to maintain → AI Winter II |
| 1990s–2000s | Statistical ML, SVMs, Bayesian | Practical, focused applications |
| 2012+ | Deep learning, GPU-powered | ImageNet breakthrough, modern AI boom |

### Where We Are Now

- **Narrow AI** — today's AI excels at specific tasks (translation, vision, game playing).
- **General AI (AGI)** — human-level intelligence across all domains. Not achieved.
- **Superintelligence** — AI surpassing human intelligence. Speculative.

Understanding the history helps separate genuine progress from hype — a critical skill for any developer working with AI.
