# Why Social Science?

## Description

Social science studies human behavior, relationships, and systems — the "people code" that runs alongside your software. For developers, it explains why users behave unpredictably, teams break down, and communication fails.

## Prerequisites

- None — just curiosity about how people and groups work

## Content / Material

### What Is Social Science?

Social science is the systematic study of human society and behavior. Its branches include psychology (individual mind), sociology (groups and systems), anthropology (culture), communication (information exchange), and economics (choice under scarcity).

Where natural sciences ask "how does the physical world work?", social sciences ask "how do people think, relate, and organize?"

### Why Developers Should Care

**Software is built by people, for people.** Every line of code is written by a human, read by another human, and used by yet another human. Ignoring the human side creates:

- **Products nobody uses** — because you didn't understand user psychology
- **Teams that don't ship** — because communication and trust broke down
- **Burnout and turnover** — because culture and relationships were neglected
- **Misunderstood requirements** — because you assumed instead of verified

**Concrete developer benefits:**

| Domain | Developer Application |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Psychology** | User behavior, motivation, cognitive biases, learning |
| **Communication** | Code reviews, docs, async writing, presentations |
| **Human relations** | Teamwork, conflict resolution, mentoring, culture |

### Branches Relevant to Software

| Branch | Focus | Why It Matters for Devs |
|--------|-------|------------------------|
| **Psychology** | Individual mind, behavior, cognition | UX design, productivity, learning, motivation |
| **Communication** | How information is exchanged | Documentation, code review, remote work |
| **Human relations** | Interpersonal relationships | Team dynamics, conflict, leadership |
| **Sociology** | Groups, organizations, systems | Company culture, org design, network effects |
| **Anthropology** | Culture, rituals, practices | Understanding user cultures, team rituals |
| **Ethics** | Right and wrong in professional practice | AI ethics, data privacy, professional responsibility |

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Social science | The study of human society and social relationships |
| Psychology | Study of mind, behavior, and mental processes |
| Sociology | Study of social groups, institutions, and systems |
