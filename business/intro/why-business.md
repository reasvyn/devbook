# Why Business?

## Description

Business is how organizations create and capture value. For developers, understanding business transforms you from a ticket-taker into a strategic partner — someone who can frame technical decisions in terms of revenue, cost, and competitive advantage.

## Content / Material

### What Is Business?

A business is an organization that creates value for customers and captures a portion of that value as profit. Every business answers three questions:

1. **What value do we create?** — product, service, or platform
2. **How do we deliver it?** — operations, supply chain, distribution
3. **How do we capture value?** — pricing, revenue model, margins

### Why Developers Should Care

**Technical skill gets you hired; business understanding gets you promoted.** The most impactful engineers are those who connect code to company outcomes.

- **Prioritization** — understanding business value helps you argue for the right work
- **Communication** — speaking in terms of revenue, cost, and risk earns executive attention
- **Product sense** — understanding users and markets makes you a better builder
- **Compensation** — understanding your company's business model helps you evaluate your own value
- **Entrepreneurship** — every startup founder needs to understand business basics

### The Business Mindset

**There is no "just technical."** Every technical decision has a business impact — positive or negative.

| Principle | Meaning | Dev Application |
|-----------|---------|-----------------|
| Value creation | Solve a real problem for customers | Build features users will actually pay for |
| Value capture | Charge appropriately for that value | Freemium, tiers, per-seat pricing |
| Competitive advantage | Do something better than anyone else | Technical moat: algorithm, data, network effects |
| Sustainable margins | Revenue > costs over time | Cloud cost optimization is a business imperative |
| Customer retention | Keep customers coming back | Reliability and UX directly impact churn |

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Value proposition | The specific benefit a product or service provides to customers |
| Revenue | Money received from selling products or services |
| Profit | Revenue minus costs |
| Margin | Profit as a percentage of revenue |
| Competitive advantage | A condition that enables a company to outperform rivals |
