### Pricing

Pricing is the most underrated product decision. It's also the hardest to change later.

**Pricing models for software:**

| Model | How It Works | Best For | Examples |
|-------|-------------|----------|----------|
| **Per-seat subscription** | Charge per user per month | B2B SaaS | Slack, GitHub, Linear |
| **Usage-based** | Charge per consumption unit | Infrastructure, APIs | AWS, Stripe, Twilio |
| **Tiered** | Fixed plans with feature limits | Most SaaS | Figma, Notion, Intercom |
| **Flat-rate** | One price, all features | Simple tools | Basecamp |
| **Freemium** | Free tier + paid upgrades | High-virality products | Dropbox, Canva |
| **Open-core** | Free OSS + paid enterprise | Developer tools | GitLab, MongoDB |
| **Enterprise** | Custom pricing for large deals | High-ACV B2B | Salesforce |

**Pricing principles:**

- **Price reflects value, not cost.** A tool that saves a company $100k/year is worth $50k/year, regardless of development cost.
- **Anchor high.** Most founders underprice. It's easier to discount than to raise prices.
- **Tier strategically.** The middle tier is usually what you expect most customers to buy. The top tier makes the middle look reasonable.
- **Charge for value.** If usage-based, align pricing to customer value (API calls, storage, users).

### Sales Motions

| Motion | How It Works | Best For | Example |
|--------|-------------|----------|---------|
| **Self-serve / PLG** | Users sign up and buy without talking to anyone | Low ACV, high volume | Figma, Notion, Slack |
| **Sales-led** | Sales team calls, demos, negotiates | High ACV ($10k+) | Salesforce, Snowflake |
| **Product-led sales** | Free product usage → sales-assisted conversion | Mid-market | Calendly, Zoom |
| **Channel / Partner** | Sell through resellers, agencies, ISVs | Indirect distribution | Adobe, Microsoft |

**Founder-led sales.** Early-stage founders must sell personally until they understand the customer so well that they can hire someone else to do it. Founder selling teaches you what the market actually needs.

**Product-led growth (PLG)** is the dominant model for modern SaaS. Users try the product themselves, experience value, and upgrade without talking to sales. PLG companies often reach $100M+ ARR with minimal sales headcount.

### Marketing Channels

| Channel | Effort | Cost | Best For |
|---------|--------|------|----------|
| **Content marketing** | High | Low ($) | SEO, authority, long-term |
| **SEO** | High | Medium ($$) | Organic traffic, sustainable |
| **Paid ads** | Medium | High ($$$) | Growth stage, proven CAC |
| **Social / Community** | Medium | Low ($) | Developer tools, B2B |
| **Email / Newsletter** | Medium | Low ($) | Lead nurturing, retention |
| **Referrals / Word of mouth** | Low | Free | Great products |
| **PR / Media** | Low | Free or agency ($$$) | Launch, brand awareness |
| **Partnerships** | High | Variable | Distribution, integrations |

**Channel selection.** Don't try all channels at once. Pick one or two that align with your customer and go deep. For dev tools, content + community is usually the play. For enterprise, content + sales-led works better.

**Content marketing is especially powerful for B2B software.** Your documentation, blog posts, tutorials, and case studies become your primary sales material. Great content generates leads for years.

### Distribution Strategy

Distribution is how the product reaches the user. For software, distribution channels include:

- **Direct** — your website, sales team, content
- **Marketplaces** — App Store, Chrome Web Store, Slack App Directory
- **Platforms** — built on top of an existing platform (Shopify apps, Salesforce AppExchange)
- **Embedded** — your product inside another product (Stripe inside Shopify)
- **Open source** — OSS adoption → enterprise conversion
- **Viral** — users invite other users (Slack, Figma, Notion)

**The distribution wedge.** Find the channel that gives you your first 100 customers at low cost. For most B2B software, that's direct outreach + content. Once you know the unit economics, you can invest in scalable channels.

### Measuring GTM Performance

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| CAC | Cost to acquire one customer |
| CAC payback | Months to recover CAC |
| LTV/CAC | Return on acquisition spend |
| Conversion rate | Lead → trial → paid |
| Time to value | Days from signup to first core action |
| Activation rate | % of signups reaching the "aha" moment |
| Channel efficiency | Revenue per channel / cost per channel |
| Sales pipeline velocity | Speed from lead to close |
