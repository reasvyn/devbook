# Why Physics Matters

## Description

Physics models how the world behaves. For developers, physics knowledge directly applies to game engines, simulations, robotics, computer graphics, and performance modeling of real-world systems.

## Content / Material

### Where Physics Meets Code

- **Game physics** — collision detection, rigid bodies, projectile motion.
- **Rendering** — ray tracing, light transport, shader math.
- **Simulations** — climate models, fluid dynamics, particle systems.
- **Robotics** — kinematics, sensor fusion, control loops.
- **Networking** — signal propagation, latency models.
- **Hardware** — thermodynamics of cooling, electromagnetism in circuit design.

### Key Areas for Developers

| Area | Application |
|------|-------------|
| **Newtonian mechanics** | Game physics, animation, simulation |
| **Electromagnetism** | Signal processing, networking, antennas |
| **Thermodynamics** | CPU/GPU cooling, data center efficiency |
| **Wave theory** | Audio processing, graphics, wireless communication |
| **Optics** | Camera sensors, computer vision, rendering |

### Intuition Over Formalism

You don't need to solve differential equations by hand. What matters is:

- Understanding how forces, energy, and motion interact.
- Knowing when to apply a simplified model vs. a precise calculation.
- Recognizing physical constraints that affect your software (latency of light, heat dissipation, sensor noise).

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Rigid body | An ideal object that does not deform under force |
| Kinematics | The study of motion without regard to forces |
| Ray tracing | A rendering technique that simulates light paths |
