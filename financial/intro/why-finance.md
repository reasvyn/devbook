# Why Finance?

## Description

Finance is the art and science of managing money. For developers, it explains how companies are valued, how fundraising works, what metrics matter to investors, and how to make data-driven financial decisions in product building.

## Content / Material

### What Is Finance?

Finance studies how individuals, companies, and institutions raise, allocate, and manage money over time. It's built on three pillars:

| Pillar | Question | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **Corporate finance** | How should a company fund and invest its operations? | Should we raise debt or equity? |
| **Investments** | How should capital be allocated to generate returns? | What is Apple stock worth? |
| **Financial markets** | How do markets price and trade financial assets? | How does the bond market work? |

### Why Developers Should Care

- **Startup compensation** — equity, options, vesting, 409A valuations directly affect your income
- **Fundraising** — understanding term sheets, dilution, and valuation helps you evaluate job offers and start your own company
- **Product economics** — LTV, CAC, payback period, and unit economics are the language of product decisions
- **Budgeting** — building a business case for headcount, tooling, or cloud spend requires financial reasoning
- **Pricing** — financial models inform subscription tiers, usage-based pricing, and enterprise deals

### The Finance Mindset

**Money has time value.** A dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow because it can be invested and earn returns. This single idea — the **time value of money** — is the foundation of all finance.

Other key principles:

- **Risk and return are linked** — higher returns require accepting higher risk
- **Cash flow matters more than profit** — a profitable company can still go bankrupt if it runs out of cash
- **Diversification reduces risk** — don't put all your eggs in one basket

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Finance | The management of money and capital |
| Time value of money | The concept that money available now is worth more than the same amount later |
| Equity | Ownership in a company |
| Valuation | The process of determining the current worth of an asset or company |
| Unit economics | The direct revenues and costs associated with a single unit of a business model |
