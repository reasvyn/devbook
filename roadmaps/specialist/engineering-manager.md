# Engineering Manager

## Description

What an engineering manager should know — leading engineering teams, coaching engineers, managing delivery, hiring, and creating a healthy engineering culture.

## Prerequisites

- Senior-level experience in software engineering
- Strong technical credibility with the teams you lead

## Learning Path

### People Management

- `[must-know]` 1:1s — cadence, structure, giving feedback
- `[must-know]` Performance reviews — setting goals, evaluating impact, calibration
- `[must-know]` Career coaching — helping engineers grow (junior → mid → senior)
- `[must-know]` Promotion readiness — identifying and advocating for high performers
- `[good-to-know]` Performance improvement plans — when and how to use them
- `[good-to-know]` Compensation and leveling — understanding market, fairness

### Team Building

- `[must-know]` Hiring — writing job descriptions, screening, interviewing, debriefing
- `[must-know]` Onboarding — structured ramp-up plans, buddy system
- `[must-know]` Team health — psychological safety, conflict resolution
- `[must-know]` Diversity and inclusion — building diverse teams, inclusive practices
- `[good-to-know]` Retention — understanding what motivates your team, addressing attrition
- `[good-to-know]` Remote and hybrid teams — async communication, time zones

### Delivery & Execution

- `[must-know]` Sprint planning and estimation — realistic commitments
- `[must-know]` Prioritization — business value vs technical debt vs growth
- `[must-know]` Roadmap planning — quarterly and yearly planning cycles
- `[must-know]` Stakeholder management — setting expectations, communicating status
- `[good-to-know]` Agile and Scrum — ceremonies, roles, continuous improvement
- `[good-to-know]` Project management — JIRA, Linear, tracking, reporting

### Technical Leadership

- `[must-know]` Technical decision-making without being the decider — asking the right questions
- `[must-know]` Architecture reviews — facilitating, ensuring quality
- `[must-know]` Code review culture — setting standards, leading by example
- `[good-to-know]` Incident leadership — coordinating response, communication
- `[good-to-know]` Technology strategy — contributing to org-wide technical direction

### Organizational Skills

- `[must-know]` Cross-team collaboration — aligning with product, design, data
- `[must-know]` Communication — written, verbal, presentations to leadership
- `[must-know]` Delegation — trusting your team, letting go of details
- `[good-to-know]` Organizational design — team topology, Conway's Law
- `[good-to-know]` Change management — introducing new processes, tools

### Emotional Intelligence

- `[must-know]` Self-awareness — understanding your biases, triggers, strengths
- `[must-know]` Empathy — understanding your team's perspective
- `[must-know]` Conflict resolution — mediating disagreements constructively
- `[good-to-know]` Resilience — managing stress, avoiding burnout
- `[good-to-know]` Radical candor — care personally, challenge directly

## Next Steps

- Director of Engineering — managing managers, organizational strategy
- VP of Engineering — company-wide engineering leadership
