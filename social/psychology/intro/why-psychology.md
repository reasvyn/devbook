# Why Psychology?

## Description

Psychology is the science of mind and behavior. For developers, it explains how users make decisions, how to design for human cognition, how to stay motivated, and how teams function.

## Content / Material

### What Is Psychology?

Psychology studies the mind — perception, cognition, emotion, motivation, personality, and behavior. It bridges biology (how the brain works) and social science (how people interact).

Major branches relevant to developers:

| Branch | Focus | Dev Relevance |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| **Cognitive** | Thinking, memory, decision-making | UX design, code comprehension, learning |
| **Social** | How people influence each other | Team dynamics, persuasion, reviews |
| **Motivational** | What drives behavior | Productivity, habit formation, gamification |
| **Behavioral** | Observable actions and reinforcement | User behavior, incentive design |

### Why Developers Should Care

- **UX and product design** — understanding perception and cognition creates intuitive interfaces
- **Productivity** — knowing how motivation and focus work helps you structure your work day
- **Learning** — understanding memory and skill acquisition accelerates your growth
- **Teamwork** — social psychology explains conflict, cooperation, and group dynamics
- **User behavior** — why users abandon carts, ignore settings, or share your product

### Key Areas for Developers

**Cognitive load.** Working memory is limited. Every element on a screen, every concept in an API, every step in a workflow consumes mental resources. Good developers minimize cognitive load for users and for themselves.

**Motivation.** Intrinsic motivation (autonomy, mastery, purpose) drives deep work more effectively than extrinsic rewards (money, status). Understanding this helps you structure your work and your team's work.

**Habit formation.** Products that become habits (cue → routine → reward) retain users. This is why the best apps feel addictive — they're designed around psychological loops.

**Heuristics and biases.** The brain uses mental shortcuts. These shortcuts explain why users make predictable "errors." Recognizing them helps you design better products and make better decisions yourself.

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Cognitive load | The mental effort required to process information |
| Intrinsic motivation | Motivation driven by internal satisfaction, not external rewards |
| Heuristic | A mental shortcut that allows quick decision-making |
| Cognitive bias | A systematic pattern of deviation from rational judgment |
