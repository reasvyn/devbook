# Why Investment?

## Description

Investment is the act of committing capital today for a return tomorrow. For developers, it explains how to evaluate assets, build wealth over time, and understand what your company's stock and equity really mean.

## Content / Material

### What Is Investing?

Investing means forgoing consumption today to earn a return that grows your future consumption. It's distinct from:

- **Speculating** — betting on short-term price movements (gambling, not investing)
- **Saving** — setting money aside with minimal risk (cash, savings accounts)
- **Spending** — consuming resources now

The investor asks: **what is this asset worth, and what return will it generate?**

### Why Developers Should Care

- **Compensation** — RSUs, stock options, and ESPPs are investment assets in your portfolio
- **Wealth building** — compounding returns over decades turns modest savings into significant wealth
- **Startup evaluation** — understanding valuation helps you evaluate early-stage job offers
- **Financial independence** — investment returns can eventually replace earned income (FIRE movement)
- **Critical thinking** — investing teaches probabilistic reasoning, risk assessment, and long-term thinking

### The Investor Mindset

**Markets are not perfectly efficient.** Prices reflect available information, but human psychology creates mispricings. You don't need to be a quant — you need discipline and a long time horizon.

Key principles:

- **Time in the market beats timing the market** — consistent investing wins over trying to predict
- **Diversification is the only free lunch** — spreading risk across assets reduces volatility
- **Your edge matters** — as a developer, you understand tech companies better than most investors
- **Don't confuse a bull market with genius** — when everyone's making money, skill is hard to measure

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Investment | Committing capital to an asset expected to generate returns over time |
| Return | The gain or loss on an investment over a period |
| Risk | The uncertainty of returns — possibility of loss |
| Diversification | Spreading investments across assets to reduce risk |
| Compounding | Earning returns on previously earned returns |
