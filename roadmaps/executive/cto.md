# Chief Technology Officer (CTO)

## Description

What a CTO should know — technical vision and strategy, building and scaling engineering organizations, aligning technology with business goals, and communicating with the board, investors, and customers.

## Prerequisites

- Deep experience in software engineering and team leadership
- Typically 10+ years in the industry, including management experience

## Learning Path

### Technical Vision & Strategy

- `🔴 CRITICAL` Defining the technical vision — 6–24 month technology roadmap
- `🔴 CRITICAL` Building vs buying vs partnering — strategic technology decisions
- `🔴 CRITICAL` Architecture governance — ensuring quality without bottlenecking
- `🔴 CRITICAL` Innovation strategy — R&D, emerging technologies, competitive moat
- `🟠 HIGH` Intellectual property strategy — patents, trade secrets, open source
- `🟠 HIGH` Platform strategy — APIs, ecosystems, developer experience

### Engineering Organization

- `🔴 CRITICAL` Org design — team topology, reporting structure, growth paths
- `🔴 CRITICAL` Hiring at scale — building recruiting machine, employer brand
- `🔴 CRITICAL` Engineering culture — values, standards, psychological safety
- `🔴 CRITICAL` Performance management at scale — calibration, promotion committees
- `🟠 HIGH` Retention strategy — career growth, compensation, engagement
- `🟠 HIGH` Remote and distributed orgs — async culture, time zone strategy

### Business Alignment

- `🔴 CRITICAL` Translating business strategy into technical strategy
- `🔴 CRITICAL` Budget planning and management — people, infrastructure, tools
- `🔴 CRITICAL` OKRs and KPI definition — engineering metrics, business impact
- `🟠 HIGH` Board presentations — technical narrative for non-technical directors
- `🟠 HIGH` Investor relations — due diligence support, technical storytelling
- `🟡 MEDIUM` M&A technical due diligence — evaluating acquisition targets

### Product Partnership

- `🔴 CRITICAL` Partnering with product leadership — shared roadmap, trade-offs
- `🔴 CRITICAL` Product-market fit from the technical side — speed, quality, scalability
- `🟠 HIGH` Customer engagement — technical sales support, executive sponsorships
- `🟡 MEDIUM` Developer relations — community, advocacy, open source presence

### Financial & Operational

- `🔴 CRITICAL` Engineering budgeting — headcount planning, tooling, cloud costs
- `🔴 CRITICAL` Cost optimization — cloud spend, infrastructure efficiency
- `🟠 HIGH` Vendor management — strategic technology partnerships
- `🟠 HIGH` Risk management — disaster recovery, compliance, security posture
- `🟡 MEDIUM` Legal and regulatory — data governance, export controls, licensing

### Leadership & Communication

- `🔴 CRITICAL` Executive communication — clarity, brevity, influence
- `🔴 CRITICAL` Leading through change — reorgs, pivots, crises
- `🔴 CRITICAL` Building leadership team — hiring VPs, directors, staff engineers
- `🟠 HIGH` Board and investor communication — quarterly updates, strategic discussions
