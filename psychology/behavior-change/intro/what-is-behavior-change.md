# What Is Behavior Change?

## Description

Behavior change is the scientific study of how and why individuals alter their behaviors, habits, thoughts, and identity structures. It draws on clinical psychology, health psychology, social psychology, and neuroscience to understand the mechanisms that drive both transient changes and lasting transformation. The field emerged from early behaviorist research (Skinner, Pavlov) and has evolved to include cognitive, social, and stage-based models that recognize change as a process rather than a single event. Key frameworks include the Transtheoretical Model (Prochaska & DiClemente), Self-Efficacy Theory (Bandura), and the COM-B Model (Michie).
