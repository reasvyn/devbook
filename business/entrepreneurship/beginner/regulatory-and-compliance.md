### Corporate Governance

As your company grows, governance requirements increase.

**Board of directors.** Legally required for C-Corps. Typical structure:

| Stage | Board Size | Composition |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Seed | 2–3 | Founders only |
| Series A | 3–5 | Founders + investors + independent |
| Series B+ | 5–7 | Founders, investors, independents, CEO |

**Board responsibilities:**
- Approve annual budget and business plan
- Oversee CEO performance and compensation
- Approve major transactions (fundraising, acquisitions)
- Ensure compliance with legal obligations
- Fiduciary duty to shareholders

**Fiduciary duties of directors:**

| Duty | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Duty of care** | Make informed decisions with reasonable diligence |
| **Duty of loyalty** | Put company interests ahead of personal interests |
| **Duty of good faith** | Act honestly and in the best interest of the company |

**Annual requirements.** Most states require: annual report filing, franchise tax payment, board meeting minutes, and shareholder meeting minutes.

### Tax Compliance

**Corporate income tax.** C-Corps pay US federal corporate income tax (21% flat rate) plus state taxes. Startups often have net operating losses (NOLs) that can offset future income.

**Sales tax.** Software sold to customers in states where you have nexus (physical presence) may require sales tax collection. SaaS is treated differently by different states — some tax it as tangible personal property, others as a service.

**International tax.** If you have customers or employees outside the US:
- VAT/GST in EU, UK, Australia, Japan
- Permanent establishment risk if employees abroad
- Transfer pricing for inter-company transactions

**Payroll tax.** If you have employees:
- Withhold income tax, Social Security, Medicare
- Pay employer share of payroll taxes
- File quarterly returns
- Provide W-2s annually

**R&D tax credit.** US startups can claim the R&D tax credit against payroll taxes (up to $250k/year). This is real cash savings for companies doing product development.

### Employment Law

**Worker classification.** The most common legal mistake startups make is misclassifying employees as independent contractors (1099 vs W-2). The IRS and state agencies use multi-factor tests. Misclassification penalties can be severe.

**If they work full-time, use your tools, and you direct their work — they're an employee.** When in doubt, classify as W-2.

**Key employment requirements:**
- Offer letters with at-will language
- Employment agreements with IP assignment
- Employee handbooks (policies on harassment, leave, remote work)
- Workers' compensation insurance
- State-required posters and notices
- Anti-discrimination and harassment training

**Termination.** At-will employment means either party can end the relationship at any time for any legal reason. But:
- Document performance issues before termination
- Never terminate for discriminatory reasons
- Pay final wages promptly (state-specific deadlines)
- Provide COBRA notice for group health plans

**International hiring.** If hiring outside your country, you need:
- Local entity or EOR (Employer of Record) — Deel, Remote, Rippling
- Compliance with local employment laws
- Tax withholding and reporting
- Visa sponsorship if relocating employees

### Data Privacy

Privacy regulations have global reach. If you have users in the EU, UK, California, or other regulated jurisdictions, compliance is mandatory.

| Regulation | Jurisdiction | Key Requirements |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| **GDPR** | EU / EEA | Consent, data minimization, right to deletion, breach notification |
| **CCPA/CPRA** | California | Right to know, delete, opt out of sale |
| **LGPD** | Brazil | Similar to GDPR |
| **PIPEDA** | Canada | Consent, purpose limitation |
| **APPI** | Japan | Consent, purpose limitation |

**GDPR essentials for startups:**
- **Lawful basis for processing** — consent, contract, legitimate interest
- **Privacy policy** — what data you collect, why, how long
- **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)** — required with vendors who process user data
- **Data subject access requests** — users can request their data
- **Breach notification** — report within 72 hours
- **Right to be forgotten** — delete user data on request
- **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** — required if processing sensitive data at scale

**Practical steps:**
- Map all data flows (what data goes where)
- Minimize data collection (don't collect what you don't need)
- Implement access controls and encryption
- Document your compliance processes
- Review vendor agreements for privacy compliance

### Securities Law

Raising money triggers securities laws — even from friends and family.

**US securities laws (Regulation D):**
- **Rule 506(b)** — raise unlimited, no general solicitation, accredited investors only
- **Rule 506(c)** — raise unlimited, general solicitation allowed, verify accredited status
- **Reg CF** — raise up to $5M from non-accredited investors (crowdfunding)

**Filing requirements:** File Form D with SEC within 15 days of first sale. Most states require additional filings (Blue Sky laws).

**International fundraising.** Different countries have different rules for soliciting investment from their residents. Consult a lawyer before taking money from investors outside your country.

### Industry-Specific Regulations

Depending on your product, additional regulations may apply:

| Industry | Regulation | Key Requirements |
|----------|-----------|------------------|
| **Fintech** | KYC/AML, Money transmitter licenses | Verify customer identity, report suspicious activity |
| **Healthtech** | HIPAA (US) | Patient data privacy, security, breach notification |
| **Edtech** | FERPA (US), COPPA (children) | Student data privacy, parental consent |
| **Govtech** | FedRAMP, SOC 2, ITAR | Security certification, export controls |
| **Adtech** | ePrivacy directive, cookie laws | Cookie consent, tracking disclosure |

**If you're in a regulated industry, hire a compliance specialist early.** The cost of getting it wrong (fines, lawsuits, business shutdown) far exceeds the cost of doing it right.

### Building a Compliance Program

1. **Identify applicable regulations** — based on geography, industry, and business model
2. **Assess risks** — likelihood and impact of non-compliance
3. **Implement controls** — policies, procedures, technical measures
4. **Train employees** — awareness and accountability
5. **Monitor and audit** — ongoing compliance verification
6. **Respond to incidents** — breach response, regulator inquiries

**When to hire legal help:**
- Incorporation — lawyer familiar with startup structures
- Fundraising — term sheet review
- First employee — employment law compliance
- First international user — GDPR assessment
- Revenue > $1M ARR — formal compliance program
