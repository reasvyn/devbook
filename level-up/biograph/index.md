# Biograph

The lives of those who built the digital world — not as distant legends, but as navigable maps for your own journey. Each biography extracts transferable principles from real careers, real failures, and real triumphs. Study them not to imitate, but to recognize patterns and draw wisdom for the path you are building.

## 1. Introduction

- [Why Study Role Models](intro/why-study-role-models.md) — the value of biographical learning and how to read these lives

## 2. Pioneers — The Founders of Computing (1810s–1950s)

Before silicon, before transistors, before even electronic computers — these individuals imagined what computation could be and laid the theoretical and mechanical foundations.

1. [Ada Lovelace](ada-lovelace.md) — the first programmer, a vision of computing beyond calculation
2. [Alan Turing](alan-turing.md) — the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence
3. [Grace Hopper](grace-hopper.md) — the first compiler and the birth of human-readable programming
4. [Hedy Lamarr](hedy-lamarr.md) — the frequency-hopping invention that underpins modern wireless

## 3. System Builders — The Architects of Modern Computing (1930s–1970s)

The individuals who built the operating systems, languages, and theoretical frameworks that define how software is written and systems are designed.

1. [Donald Knuth](donald-knuth.md) — *The Art of Computer Programming* and the formalization of algorithm analysis
2. [Margaret Hamilton](margaret-hamilton.md) — software engineering and the code that landed on the Moon
3. [Dennis Ritchie](dennis-ritchie.md) — the C language and the Unix operating system
4. [Ken Thompson](ken-thompson.md) — Unix co-creator, Go language, and the philosophy of simplicity
5. [Bjarne Stroustrup](bjarne-stroustrup.md) — the creation of C++ and the principle of zero-overhead abstraction

## 4. Visionaries — The Shapers of the Digital Age (1950s–present)

Those who transformed computing from an academic and industrial tool into a global platform for communication, creativity, and commerce.

1. [Steve Jobs](steve-jobs.md) — the intersection of technology and liberal arts
2. [Tim Berners-Lee](tim-berners-lee.md) — the invention of the World Wide Web and the fight for an open internet
3. [Linus Torvalds](linus-torvalds.md) — Linux, Git, and the power of open-source collaboration
4. [Guido van Rossum](guido-van-rossum.md) — Python and the philosophy of readable code
